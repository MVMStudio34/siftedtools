## Why GEO tracking matters now

Traditional SEO measures where you rank on Google. That world is shrinking. Gartner expects a 25% drop in traditional search volume by 2026, and when AI Overviews appear, the click-through rate to actual sites collapses. More and more, the AI's answer *is* the answer, with no click to your store.

For e-commerce this is concrete. A buyer no longer types "best wireless earbuds under £100" into Google and scrolls. They ask ChatGPT, and ChatGPT names three brands. If you're one of the three, you're in the consideration set. If you're not, you lost the sale before the shopper ever saw a product page, and you have no log file or analytics dashboard telling you it happened. GEO tracking is how you make that invisible loss visible. The UK is the second-largest Shopify Plus market in the world, and British and US merchants are exactly the buyers shifting research into AI chat first.

One honest caveat up front: these tools *measure*, they don't *optimize* for you. Watching your visibility score move is not the same as moving it. The content structuring, schema and authority-building work still has to happen, by you or your agency.

## Otterly: the best value entry point

*Our pick for starting AI visibility tracking in month one.*

Otterly is the tool I recommend to start with. It's the most affordable in the category and it covers the essentials without the complexity or cost of an enterprise platform.

**What it does well.** Price is the headline: from around $25 to $29 a month with a free trial, it's the only tool here that a freelancer or a small Shopify store can adopt without a second thought. It automatically tracks your mentions and citations across the engines that matter most: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews and AI Mode, and Copilot. A genuinely smart feature converts your existing SEO keywords into conversational AI prompts, so you're not starting from a blank page. The built-in GEO audit points you at how to optimize your content to get cited more. Setup is quick: usable data in 48 hours, versus the week or two heavier tools demand. Gartner named it a Cool Vendor in 2025, which tells you the category is maturing.

**Where it falls short.** Cheaper plans cover a narrower set of engines, so Meta AI, Grok or DeepSeek aren't always included. That's a fair trade given ChatGPT and Perplexity carry most real-world usage. The dashboard gets busy once you track a lot of prompts or projects. Sentiment analysis is good for the general trend but worth spot-checking on the finer cases. Competitor tracking exists but it's basic compared with Peec.

**Best if:** you're a solo founder, a small DTC team or an agency, you want to prove this category is worth the spend, and the budget is tight.

## Peec AI: the competitor and share-of-voice specialist

Peec AI sits a notch above Otterly in depth and positions itself as the mid-market analytics platform, built for marketing teams and agencies that want quality data without the enterprise price tag.

**What it does well.** The unlimited-seats model is a real advantage: the whole team can monitor, collaborate and act on the data with no per-user billing, which makes it economical as you grow. Tracking is daily. Its sharpest feature is benchmarking: Peec is genuinely strong at showing your share of voice against named competitors, so you see not just whether you're mentioned but how often relative to the brands you're losing to. It also draws a clean line many tools blur, separating brand mentions (when you're named) from source citations (when your content is used without your name attached). Pitch Workspaces let agencies share visibility reports with prospects to win business. The interface is clean and there's a Looker Studio connector. The client list (Wix, ElevenLabs, Chanel) suggests it holds up at scale.

**Where it falls short.** Peec is lighter on truly actionable next-step advice: it tells you where you stand better than what to do about it. Base plans track only three engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews); Gemini, Claude, DeepSeek, Llama and Grok cost extra. Reporting is more summarized and abstract than Profound's, so you get the trend but lose the per-answer detail. Pricing in euros adds a little variability for teams billing outside the eurozone.

**Best if:** you're a marketing team or agency that lives and dies by competitive benchmarking and share of voice, you want unlimited seats, and you manage more than one brand or client.

## Profound: enterprise-grade GEO analytics

Profound is the enterprise reference. It's the deepest, most complete tool in the category, built for large teams with the resources to use the data fully. That power comes at a price.

**What it does well.** The analytical depth is the best on the market. Where Peec summarizes, Profound shows the exact text of AI answers with your brand highlighted in context, so you understand precisely how the systems interpret your content. Profound Workflows automate content operations for the AI-search era. Sentiment analysis tracks whether the AI describes you positively, neutrally or negatively. The platform is SOC 2 Type II certified and HIPAA compliant, which matters in regulated sectors. It's also the best at catching secondary engines, picking up mentions on Grok and DeepSeek that others miss.

**Where it falls short.** Price is a genuine barrier. The base plan often tracks only ChatGPT, and multi-engine coverage needs a markedly more expensive tier. Add the internal headcount needed to analyze the data, produce content and build authority, and the real total cost climbs hard. There's no free trial, only demos, so you have to commit to test it. And like every tool here, it diagnoses without executing: it shows you you're invisible, but the optimization work is still yours.

**Best if:** you're a larger brand or enterprise with a dedicated budget and analysts to act on the data, you need SOC 2 compliance, or you want the finest per-answer detail and the most complete multi-engine coverage.

## Which one based on your situation

**Solo founder or small store, new to AI visibility.** Free trial then Otterly Lite. You find out whether you show up in AI recommendations without a heavy spend. The rational starting point, full stop.

**Growing DTC brand taking this seriously.** Otterly Standard or Peec AI Starter. Daily tracking, several engines, sentiment. Pick Otterly if price leads; pick Peec the moment competitor benchmarking and share of voice are what you actually care about.

**Agency managing AI visibility for several clients.** Peec AI, for the unlimited seats and Pitch Workspaces that help you win and report on clients. Otterly is the fallback if your clients are smaller and price-sensitive.

**Larger brand or enterprise with budget and a team.** Profound, for the analytical depth and the most complete multi-engine coverage. The cost is justified only if you have the people to act on what it finds.

The trap cuts both ways. Buy Profound before you have a team to use it and you're paying enterprise prices for a dashboard nobody opens. Stay on a spot-check spreadsheet too long and your competitors quietly own the AI answer while you're blind to it.
