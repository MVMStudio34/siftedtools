## Klaviyo: the standard when email is a revenue channel

*Our pick for stores scaling email and SMS as a real revenue line.*

Klaviyo is the global benchmark for e-commerce email, and on Shopify that's not marketing fluff. The integration is the deepest on the market, full stop. Every Shopify event — product view, add-to-cart, abandoned checkout, purchase, return, loyalty tier — is available as an automation trigger with fine-grained conditions. Nothing else gets this close to your store data.

The segmentation is where the money is. You can build dynamic segments that would be impossible elsewhere: repeat buyers of a specific category, above a given average order value, who haven't purchased in 30 days but opened at least two of your last five emails. Point those segments at the right flow and your revenue-per-send climbs. That's the whole reason serious stores pay the premium. The UK is the second-largest Shopify Plus market in the world, and high-growth British and US merchants overwhelmingly standardise on Klaviyo for exactly this reason.

The AI is real, not a checkbox. Klaviyo AI predicts the best send time per contact, generates segments from behaviour automatically, and suggests A/B variants on subject lines and content. None of it replaces a strategy, but it removes guesswork from the parts that are genuinely hard to eyeball.

The trade-off is price. Klaviyo bills on active contacts and scales fast. At 10,000 contacts you can be looking at several hundred dollars a month, and that only pays back if email drives meaningful revenue. The other honest caveat: the depth is wasted if nobody on your team builds the advanced segments. A store that uses Klaviyo like a basic newsletter tool is overpaying for a Ferrari to do the school run.

**Best if:** you're past roughly 5,000 active contacts on Shopify, email is a real revenue channel, and you have someone who'll actually use the segmentation and flows.

## Brevo: 80% of the value for far less

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) is the best-value all-in-one in this comparison, and the right default for budget-conscious or growing stores. The headline is the free plan: 300 emails a day, genuinely usable in production rather than a teaser. A store with a few hundred contacts running a weekly newsletter plus a couple of automated sequences can sit on the free tier for months.

Where Brevo earns its keep is the suite. Email, SMS, WhatsApp, live chat and a light CRM in one affordable subscription means you're not stitching together three tools and three bills. For multichannel stores that want SMS and WhatsApp without Klaviyo money, nothing here matches it on value. EU and UK merchants also get GDPR handled cleanly, with EU data hosting that simplifies your privacy documentation — worth a line for British stores, though all three tools are compliant so it isn't decisive on its own.

The weaknesses are exactly the flip side of Klaviyo's strengths. Behavioural segmentation is standard rather than advanced: you can segment on purchase and engagement behaviour, but the options are fewer and the builder less fluid. The Shopify integration works but isn't as deep, which shows when you want to trigger automations on specific store events. The built-in AI does subject and copy suggestions but trails Klaviyo AI on behavioural prediction.

The mental model is simple. Brevo gives you most of what most stores need, at a price that leaves budget for ads, content and stock. You only outgrow it when segmentation depth or Shopify event granularity becomes a real ceiling — and many stores never hit that.

**Best if:** you're growing, watching the budget, or you want email plus SMS and WhatsApp in one affordable suite without overpaying for depth you won't use yet.

## Omnisend: the Shopify-native middle ground

*Our pick for getting e-commerce automation live fast.*

Omnisend occupies the space between the other two. Less powerful than Klaviyo on advanced segmentation, less economical than Brevo over the long run, but with one clear advantage: its prebuilt e-commerce workflows are the fastest to launch on the market.

Abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back, birthday, back-in-stock — they all exist as prebuilt flows you can switch on in a few clicks, copy templates included. For a marketing team with no deep email expertise that wants to be operational in an afternoon and measuring results the same week, that's a real shortcut. The interface is cleaner than Klaviyo's and more modern than Brevo's, and setup support for the automations is included from the entry paid plan.

The caveats are honest ones. Advanced segmentation isn't the strong suit: it covers the common cases but falls short of Klaviyo for combined behavioural conditions. Cost climbs from around 1,000 contacts, so factor it into your unit economics rather than assuming it stays cheap. And while Omnisend is genuinely Shopify-native, it doesn't have Klaviyo's event depth once your flows get sophisticated.

**Best if:** you want strong e-commerce automation live quickly, you're a mid-market store wanting a balance of power and simplicity, or you're torn between Brevo and Klaviyo and want a faster setup.

## GetResponse: the generalist, not the specialist

GetResponse deserves a mention for stores that want an all-in-one marketing platform rather than a specialist email tool. Beyond email it bundles landing pages, full funnel automation, webinars and AI tools that generate marketing assets from a few prompts. That's a coherent offer if you'd otherwise pay for three separate subscriptions, and the platform has 25-plus years on the market behind it.

The problem for e-commerce is focus. GetResponse isn't built Shopify-first: its store integration doesn't go as deep as Klaviyo's, and for pure e-commerce email it doesn't beat Brevo on value. It's a capable generalist that happens to do email, not an e-commerce email engine. If you sell courses or content alongside physical products, or you value consolidating webinars and pages into one tool, it makes sense. For email-led e-commerce, the other three are sharper.

**Best if:** you want one platform for email, landing pages, automation and webinars, and you sell more than just physical products.

## Which one based on your situation

**Just starting, under 500 contacts.** Brevo, free plan. You can test in real conditions for months without paying. Build your welcome sequence and your abandoned-cart flow, measure the revenue, and you'll know exactly when to upgrade.

**Growing, 500 to 5,000 contacts.** Brevo, paid plan, in most cases. The value is still the best on the market at this volume, and the money you save versus Klaviyo is better spent on creative, content and stock. The exception: if email already drives serious revenue and you're hungry for segmentation depth, start evaluating Klaviyo now.

**Scaling Shopify store, 5,000 to 20,000 contacts.** This is the real decision point. If your monthly email revenue is high — think four figures and climbing — Klaviyo pays back through better segmentation and deeper Shopify triggers. If email is still a minor channel, stay on Brevo and invest in the quality of your sequences first. The price difference only makes sense when you'll use the depth.

**Past 20,000 active contacts.** Klaviyo, no hesitation. At this scale the gap in power shows up as missed revenue, and the premium is clearly justified.

**Marketing team with no email expertise.** Omnisend. The prebuilt flows get you live in hours and proving value fast. Migrate to Klaviyo later when the team has mastered the channel.

**Multichannel store wanting email, SMS and WhatsApp in one place.** Brevo. It's the most complete affordable suite for this. Klaviyo has SMS but no native WhatsApp; Omnisend has SMS but fewer channels.

The classic trap cuts both ways: buy Klaviyo before email is a real channel and you pay for power you can't use, or cling to Brevo so long that weak segmentation quietly caps your revenue. ## The verdict

There's no universal winner, there's a winner for your stage and your revenue.

Run **Klaviyo** if email and SMS are a genuine revenue channel and you'll use the segmentation, flows and Shopify data that justify its price — it's the standard for scaling stores for a reason. Run **Brevo** if you want roughly 80% of that value for far less, with SMS and WhatsApp in the same affordable suite, which covers most growing stores comfortably. Reach for **Omnisend** when speed-to-live matters more than depth, and **GetResponse** only when you genuinely need an all-in-one platform beyond email. The only wrong moves are paying for Klaviyo's depth before you'll use it, or letting Brevo's ceiling cap your revenue long after you've outgrown it.
