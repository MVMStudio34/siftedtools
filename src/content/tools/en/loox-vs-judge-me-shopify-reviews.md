If you run a Shopify store and you're picking a reviews app, here's the short version: **Judge.me** is the right call for most stores, **Loox** wins when photo and video reviews actually drive your conversion, and **Yotpo** only makes sense at scale when you need reviews bundled with loyalty and SMS. All three are good. They're built for different stages and different categories of the same job. For the wider stack, see our [complete guide to AI tools for e-commerce](/en/best-ai-tools-ecommerce/).

Most "Loox vs Judge.me vs Yotpo" pages hedge. This one commits. It's based on running all three on live stores, not paraphrasing their pricing pages.

## The verdict at a glance

Judge.me is the best value for the majority of Shopify stores, full stop. Loox is the better tool when visual social proof is core to your category. Yotpo is the enterprise option, powerful but overkill and pricey unless you're running it as a full marketing suite. The line between them isn't quality. It's what your category needs and how much of a marketing stack you're trying to consolidate.

## Judge.me: the default value pick

*Our pick for most Shopify stores.*

Judge.me is the app I recommend by default, and the reasoning is blunt: it does roughly 95% of what Loox does, at a fraction of the cost, and its free plan is a real offer rather than a marketing teaser. For a store that needs reviews to work and doesn't need a content shoot per review, that's the whole decision.

What it does well: the Forever Free plan covers unlimited review requests, photo reviews, and SEO rich snippets, the bit that puts star ratings into your Google results. That last point matters more than people credit. Rich snippets behind a paywall is how the other two nudge you onto paid tiers, and Judge.me just includes them. The Awesome plan at around fifteen dollars a month adds video reviews, Q&A, and cross-store sync, which is genuinely useful if you run more than one Shopify brand off one back office. The request-email builder is deeper than Loox's, with multi-step flows and the option to write raw HTML or switch Judge.me's sends off entirely and run everything through Klaviyo.

Where it falls short: the default widget is denser and more text-led than Loox. To match Loox's polish you'll spend time in custom CSS or lean on community templates. The Google Shopping ratings connection needs more manual feed work than Loox or Yotpo, which have it native. Support is fast but text-only.

**Best if:** you're bootstrapped, budget-conscious, technical enough to tweak a widget, or simply selling in a category where the star count matters more than the customer's photo. That's most stores.

## Loox: visual social proof done properly

Loox made a clear bet: be the best at visual reviews, photo and video, and charge a premium for it. For its target segment, the bet pays off. If a customer photo is what closes the sale in your category, Loox is doing real conversion work that Judge.me's default widget doesn't.

What it does well: the visual widgets are the most polished on the market out of the box. Photo carousels, mosaics, premium star badges, all tuned for conversion with zero CSS. Photo and video reviews are on every plan, not gated behind the top tier. Google Shopping ratings are native on paid plans. Its newer AI layer auto-sorts the strongest visual reviews first and translates reviews for international buyers, which is a quiet win for cross-border stores. There's a built-in referrals system on higher plans, so satisfied customers become a small acquisition channel inside the same tool.

Where it falls short: there's no free plan, so the trial commitment is higher. Pricing sits at the top of the market and only justifies itself once the conversion lift from visual proof outweighs the spend, which for low-volume stores it may not. Below a certain order volume, Loox Beginner can actually undercut Judge.me Awesome; above it, Judge.me pulls decisively ahead on cost. So "Loox is expensive" is only half true, the crossover depends on your volume.

**Best if:** you sell fashion, beauty, home or anything where the look of the review is part of the product, you want a premium feel without touching CSS, and visual social proof is core to how you convert.

## Yotpo: the suite, not the app

Yotpo plays a tier up. It's not really a reviews app anymore; it's a full e-commerce marketing suite, and its price and complexity put it out of reach for the average store. Judge it as a suite, because that's the only way it makes sense.

What it does well: the native Google Shopping and Google Seller Ratings integration is arguably the most complete on the market. The suite bundles reviews, loyalty, SMS marketing and UGC, so a brand that was juggling four or five separate tools can consolidate. The certified partner ecosystem is dense, which helps when you need implementation support rather than a help doc.

Where it falls short: the entry price around seventy-five dollars a month, scaling quickly from there, makes it overkill for the majority of stores under a thousand orders a month. The feature depth brings a real learning curve, some modules like SMS are geo-restricted, and the interface stays denser than Loox or Judge.me despite improvements. If you only need reviews, you're paying for a suite you won't use.

**Best if:** you're mid-market or enterprise, past roughly a thousand orders a month, and the return comes from consolidation, reviews plus loyalty plus SMS plus UGC, rather than reviews alone.

## Which one based on your situation

**You're just launching (under ~50 orders a month).** Judge.me free plan, no hesitation. Spend the money you saved on a bit of CSS to get the widget looking decent. You can move to Loox later if visual proof becomes critical.

**You're growing (50 to 200 orders) with a visual brand.** Loox Beginner is likely justified here. The conversion you gain from polished photo widgets offsets the premium. If your category doesn't live on aesthetics, stay on Judge.me Awesome and pocket the difference.

**You're established (200 to 1,000 orders).** Judge.me Awesome becomes dramatically cheaper than Loox at this volume. If you started on Judge.me and invested in CSS, stay put. If you started on Loox and the price gap now grates, migration is a few hours of work.

**You're mid-market (1,000+ orders) or multichannel.** Evaluate Yotpo seriously, but for the suite, not the reviews. The ROI is in centralising loyalty, SMS and UGC. If you don't need that, Judge.me or Loox still does the review job better per pound spent.

**You run several Shopify brands.** Judge.me Awesome with cross-store sync is hard to beat at fifteen dollars a brand. Loox doesn't handle multi-store as cleanly.

The classic trap cuts both ways. Buy Yotpo before you have a suite to fill and you're burning margin on idle modules. Cling to a free widget when visual proof is what your category sells on, and you're leaving conversion on the table. Match the app to your category and stage, not to a feature list.

## The verdict

There's no universal best Shopify reviews app, but there is a clear default. **Judge.me** wins for the majority: it does what matters, the free plan is real, and rich snippets come included. Choose **Loox** when the customer photo is part of the product and visual proof drives your conversion, fashion, beauty, home. Reach for **Yotpo** only at scale, when reviews are one module in a loyalty-and-SMS suite you'll actually use. The only wrong moves are paying Yotpo prices for a reviews widget, or starving a visual brand on a text-first free plan. Pick on merit, match the app to your category, and don't buy ahead of where your store actually is.
